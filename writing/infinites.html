The mathematical concept of the set of all real numbers has always been interesting to me. First of all, there's the aesthetics of the notation. Let's not deny that the infinity symbol conveys an elegance and symmetry (well, depending on the glyph) that's artistically pleasing and thought-provoking. I wonder if it's strange that the aesthetics of the notation are what captivated me about the concept before anything else. But there's also what the notation represents: a limitless stretch of potential numbers extending from either side of the number 0. This limitlessness is both intriguing and kind of unsettling. There's a never ending list of potential values into numbers that we don't have names for yet but also a lack of closure and completeness inherent to that.
It's not unlike the state of the world around us today.

We live in a world of infinite possibility. Information moves across continents in mere milliseconds. Such movement would have taken days, months, years, generations before. The potential this unlocks cannot be overstated. Previously held back by what the structure of society and technology could bear, we can now move forward at a breathtaking pace. Want to learn a new skill? The friction has been reduced thanks to a plethora of free resources that'll either get you started or get you a decent portion of the way there. Building a brand once necessitated coordinating with a team of PR people. Now a brand can be as simple as a vibe communicated over various short notes. People pay you to be you. The barrier to further financial success has been reduced for those with the know-how, determination, or support. (Obligatory note: nothing I'm saying here is financial advice.) Platforms pay you just to be right. Or to be lucky. Or both. Earlier this decade, we made available to the general public a tool that amplifies all of these possibilities further. With every successive iteration of artificial intelligence information, knowledge, and even execution are brought more and more within reach.

We live in a world of infinite risk. The infinitely quick movement of information means that it is easier to be taken advantage of. Decreased friction from accessing knowledge means that those who don't display certain qualities above and beyond competency risk becoming unhireable. And then there's a different kind of barrier to development and learning that comes in the form of the infinite amount of entertainment we have at our fingertips. Satisfying your brain has become infinitely cheap. Effort and ambition have become infinitely expensive. The growing necessity of maintaining a personal brand means that all of your traits, even those that would resonate negatively, are amplified to potentially millions. The same platforms that "pay you to be right" can vanish infinite amounts of money if you're wrong. In fact, it's not the platforms that are paying users, but the losers who are paying winners. The tool we've made amplifies all of these risks further with each successive release. With the best that AI has to offer being locked behind a monetary barrier, some have feared the creation of an infinitely lasting "underclass" of people.

For those of us who are starting at or close to zero, these are intriguing and unsettling times. How do we navigate this rush of outcomes and obstacles? One thing is for sure. Despite the infinite risk, it isn't feasible to simply remain at zero. The "safe option" doesn't work because innovation is constantly pushing those who don't adapt further down the number line. This is a good thing, though. It's a spurring on to try to do great things that we otherwise wouldn't get during the good times strong people have created. Though in a state of apparent infinite comfort, we must become aware of the stakes at hand and realize that this is it. And by "it" I don't mean the end; rather, the thing. This is it. Behold the age of infinites.
